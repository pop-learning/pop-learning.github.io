Design And Build For HTML Email
GLOSSARY:
Explanations of HTML email related terms

Glossary


Call-to-action
A call-to-action is a prompt to the reader to take a specific action. It is visually represented by a text link or button.		 Click-bait
Click-bait is typically a misleading headline link that encourages readers to navigate to low-value or irrelevant content.
Conversion
A conversion is the completion of a desired action, e.g. a marketing email that a recipient to click-through and make a purchase.		 CSS Positioning
The CSS position property offers four types of positioning methods for HTML elements; static, relative, fixed, and absolute.
Inbox version
Inbox version refers to when the email is viewed in the email client inbox.		 Microsite
The back pages of an email are referred to as the microsite. The microsite pages are displayed in the browser, not the email client inbox.
Nielsen Norman group
The Nielsen Norman group are an authority in the field of user experience and usability testing.		 Tap-targets
Tap-target refers to a call-to-action on a touchscreen mobile platform.
UX
UX refers to User Experience. UX design is the process of designing intuitive and easy-to-use products.		 UI
UI refers to User Interface. UI design is the graphic design of the interactive screens or visuals.
Viewport
The viewport is the visible area of a web page on a desktop or mobile platform.		 Web Version
Web version refers to when the email is viewed in the browser rather than the email client.
