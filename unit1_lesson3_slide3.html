Email Clients and Market Share

Email Platforms

Email can be opened on three different platforms: Desktop, Webmail, and Mobile.

When creating email for internal communications (emails sent within an organisation, e.g. from management to employees), your audience will typically use only one or two email clients, and you may be given an indication of the expected platform.

For example:
  • Approximately 70% of the audience are on desktop PCs and they use Outlook 2013
  • 30% are on mobile
  • They all use iPhones as standard with the native mail app


Mobile refers to phones and tablet devices. Desktop refers to an email client accessed through a desktop application. Examples would be Outlook, Apple Mail, Thunderbird, and IBM Notes (formerly Lotus notes). Webmail refers to an email client accessed through a browser. Examples would be Gmail, Yahoo! Mail, AOL Mail, Office365, and Outlook.com.
