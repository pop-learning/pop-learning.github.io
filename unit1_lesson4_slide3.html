External vs. Internal Communication

Internal Communications

Internal communications refer to communications within an organisation, e.g. from management to employees. Some examples are: a general employee newsletter, a message from the CEO, or Sales updates.

Some level of audience information is typically provided for internal communications, what email clients they are using and what the expected platforms are. You may not have exact metrics, but typically one or two email clients are used as standard across an organisation.

For internal communications, desktop and mobile are likely to be the most common platforms. The different roles of employees within the organisation can dictate what platforms they are likely to use. Floor, production, or sales employees (staff who are not desk-based) are likely to use a mobile platform. It's important to ask these questions when gathering information about the target audience.
