Principles of Good Design

Serif vs Sans-serif


For smaller body copy sans-serif font is thought to be more legible as it scales better on smaller resolutions. There are many conflicting opinions on the legibility of serif vs. sans-serif fonts. Generally serif fonts are thought to provide more clarity: take the 'illinois' text example on the right, which shows a comparison of Arial and Georgia fonts. The serifs help to differentiate between the uppercase and lowercase letters.

Alex Poole, a user-experience consultant, references legibility studies in his article Which Are More Legible: Serif or Sans Serif Typefaces?

Customers may ask your for your recommendations on font choices, but most often the font choice will usually be dictated by the customer's brand guidelines.
