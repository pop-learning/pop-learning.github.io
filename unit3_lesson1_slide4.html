Principles of Good Design

Colour Use

Click the thumbnails to view full–size images.

There are numerous ways to use colour. Strong colours add contrast and can be used to create stand out call-to-action links. Colour-blocking can be used to highlight feature article areas, divide content, and make certain areas pop. Use colour tints to help to soften colour blocks. Use colours that complement the imagery. When building an email template you may not have much control over the content. In this case, try to offer colour-change options for the customer. Don't be afraid to take a minimalist approach to colour. Often less is more. Use colour for subtle accents in your email design. Consider a monochrome palette to create a sleek sophisticated look. A neutral colour palette will work well with most images, and is often preferred when customers are seeking a more corporate look and feel for their email design.
