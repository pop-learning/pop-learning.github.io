Principles of Good Design

Visual Weight


Visual weight is the perceived weight of a visual element. It is a measure of how much a visual element draws the reader's eye. Visual weight can be measured by size, colour, value, position, texture, shape, and orientation.

Large elements have more visual weight than small elements.
Texture gives an element a three-dimensional appearance, suggesting greater mass and visual weight.
Dark colours are perceived as heavier than light colours.
Warm colours advance into the foreground, while cool colours recede into the background.
Objects with a regular shape appear heavier than objects with an irregular shape. The regular shape is perceived as a whole object, while the irregular shape is seen as missing it's full mass.
