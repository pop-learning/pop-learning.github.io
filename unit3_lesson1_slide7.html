Principles of Good Design

Balance


"A balanced composition feels right. It feels stable and aesthetically pleasing. While some of its elements might be focal points and attract your eye, no one area of the composition draws your eye so much that you can't see the other areas."

There are different types of balance:

Symmetrical
balance
Asymmetrical

Radial
balance
Mosaic
balance
Symmetrical balance: Equal weight on equal sides of a design. The two sides mirror one another. This is not the most visually intriguing form of balance as it is rather predictable and suggests a tone of formality.
Asymmetrical balance: A number of small objects can balance out one larger object. One side of the design contains a visually heavier element, which is balanced by a number of visually lighter elements on the other side. Asymmetrical balance offers more visual variety.
Radial balance: When elements in the design radiate from a common centre point, e.g. rays of sunlight for example. Radial balance draws your eye toward the center point. It is difficult to achieve in email, as the layering of content over background images is not supported. Radial balance is more suited to custom graphics.
Mosaic balance: When a design lacks clear focal points, and the design elements share an equal emphasis. It is about the balance of repetition. Repeating elements of equal weight throughout the design creates a sense of balance.
