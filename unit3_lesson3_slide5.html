Lesson 3: Design Call Communications

Common Customer Questions

Select an appropriate response to the customer's question. This is not a quiz,
and you can click on each potential answer to see all possible feedback.

A. A successful email is less about the placement of call-to-action buttons and more about the quality of the email content. Relevant and interesting material will motivate the reader to scroll.
B. Scrolling has become an intuitive behaviour. Apple is so confident of the natural inclination to scroll that their newer operating systems have removed the scrollbar by default.
C. The area 'above the fold' is valuable space suited for priority information, but do not crowd too much content into this area.
All of these answers are correct, and ideally they should be combined together.

You want to prevent the customer from becoming fixated on placing content above the fold, but you should also acknowledge that it is an area immediately visible to the reader and is a suitable location for priority information.
