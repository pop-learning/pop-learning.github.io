Email Content Management

Content Management

With the average attention span of 11 seconds, it's important to keep your email content concise, write effective summaries and use interesting visuals where possible. Don't add unnecessary 'filler' content as email templates are designed to be flexible and accommodate differing amounts of content per email.

"If the content is dull or irrelevant, it doesn't matter how you align it, edit it or phrase it. As Bill Bernbach said, it's the art of persuasion. Put some meat on that bone."
- Marcus Johnson

Shorter articles are easier for the reader's eye to quickly scan. Use 'read more' links to link out to longer content. Create a hierarchy of content and place your most important priority article at the top of the newsletter above the fold.

Visually divide the content with bullets, dividers, and section headers. Secondary articles can be designed as hyperlinked article titles by removing the summary text and 'read more' call-to-action.
